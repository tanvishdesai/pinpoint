# Venue Notes

Target venue: Ain Shams Engineering Journal, Elsevier / ScienceDirect.

## Official guide observations

- The journal uses double-anonymized review. The submission package should include a title page with author details and an anonymized manuscript without identifying information.
- Manuscripts should use clearly defined numbered sections and numbered subsections.
- The guide lists the expected research-article structure as Introduction, Material and methods, Theory/calculation when applicable, Results, Discussion, and Conclusions.
- The abstract should be concise and factual, state purpose, principal results, and major conclusions, and avoid references unless essential.
- The guide allows up to 6 keywords.
- Figures and tables should be placed near the relevant text, with captions and matching in-text citations.
- Tables should avoid vertical rules and shading.
- The journal requests declarations, competing-interest disclosure, funding information, CRediT author roles, and a generative-AI disclosure when applicable.

## Recent venue/style scan

The following recent Ain Shams Engineering Journal articles were used as style anchors because they are computational, machine-learning, explainability, or attention-oriented:

1. Kaya and Alhajj, "An integrated explainable framework for multimodal deepfake detection across image, audio, and video data," 2026.
   - Same problem family as PinPoint.
   - Uses a direct engineering framing: limitation, proposed framework, benchmark datasets, accuracy numbers, and interpretability claim in the abstract.
   - The introduction motivates real-world risk first, then narrows to unimodal and XAI limitations.

2. "DeepPQDs-DWT-STNet: A novel DWT-ST and hybrid deep learning framework for power quality disturbance classification," 2025.
   - Strong example of presenting a named model, datasets, noise robustness, and deployment timing in the abstract.
   - Reports multiple test conditions rather than one headline number.

3. "Understanding chemical ecotoxicity through interpretable ensemble learning," 2025.
   - Relevant XAI style anchor.
   - Presents predictive performance and explanation tools as paired evidence.

4. "DEAM-XCN: Alzheimer's disease detection using distributed ensemble activation function based explainable convolutional neural network," 2025.
   - Example of an explainable CNN article using accuracy, precision, recall, and cross-validation results.
   - Shows that the venue accepts explainability as a main contribution when tied to a practical detection problem.

5. "Autoencoder-based OFDM-IM with joint index-symbol classification via attention mechanisms," 2026.
   - Relevant for attention and multi-task loss framing.
   - Uses highlights, concise abstract, and a clear task-specific loss contribution.

## Synthesis for PinPoint v2

The v2 draft mirrors the venue by using a simple Elsevier manuscript structure, numbered sections, concise tables without vertical rules, and a practical engineering tone. The old Springer-style "Proposed Method" and "Comprehensive XAI Framework" wording was converted into "Materials and methods," "Explainable AI evaluation protocol," and "Experiments and results." Claims were made more conservative where protocols differ across datasets, especially in the comparison table.
